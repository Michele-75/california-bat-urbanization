VIIRS (Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite) Data Notes

-Time frame is 2012-2024 based on VIIRS data availability: https://eogdata.mines.edu/products/vnl/#annual_v2

-VNL=Visible Nighttime Lights

-Annual VNL products are:

*yearly summaries of nighttime light intensity

*derived from VIIRS Day/Night Band (DNB)

*processed to remove: clouds, moonlight, sunlight, fires, aurora, background noise

-Value we care about: average radiance (nW/cm²/sr) (our quantitive proxy for light pollution)

-vcm = VIIRS Cloud Mask "vcm version" means:
*only cloud-free pixels are used

*stray light contamination is excluded

*this is good and standard

-A Satellite Instrument: VIIRS is an instrument on the Suomi NPP, NOAA-20, and NOAA-21 satellites.
-A Sensor for Low Light: Its special Day/Night Band (DNB) is designed to detect faint light, even moonlight, enabling the mapping of human activity at night. 


“Annual nighttime light intensity was quantified using the VIIRS Annual Visible Nighttime Lights (VNL) products produced by the Earth Observation Group (EOG), Payne Institute for Public Policy. These products provide globally consistent annual average radiance composites derived from cloud-free VIIRS Day/Night Band observations. Version 2.1 data were used for the period 2012–2021, and Version 2.2 data were used for 2022–2024, reflecting updates to processing methodology and the incorporation of multi-satellite observations in later years. Version changes were aligned with official EOG release boundaries, and no year was analyzed using mixed processing versions.”

“For 2012, the Annual VNL v2.1 composite spanning April 2012–March 2013 was used, consistent with EOG recommendations for constructing a full-year baseline in the first year of VIIRS observations.”
